# 💬 The Conversation Guide
### How to have the conversations that matter most

> A single-file, browser-based guide to 20 of the most important and most avoided conversations in human life — with step-by-step guidance, real scripts, and age-specific language for every stage of life from 3 to adulthood.

---

## 📖 Overview

**The Conversation Guide** is built on a simple premise: most relationship problems aren't caused by bad intentions — they're caused by people not knowing how to say the thing that needs to be said. Whether it's a child learning to tell a friend they were hurt, a teenager setting a limit with a peer, or an adult finally having the conversation they've been avoiding for years — this guide helps.

Every topic covers the full lifespan. Pick a conversation, then choose the age tab that fits. The language, examples, steps, and scripts all adapt — but the emotional truth underneath stays the same.

Designed for use in **schools, counseling offices, therapy practices, parenting, and personal growth** — and simple enough that anyone can use it alone.

---

## ✨ Features

### 🗂️ One Unified Topic List
- All 20 conversation topics are in a single searchable sidebar — no mode switching, no separate kids vs. adults sections
- Each topic shows which age groups are covered at a glance
- Sidebar is searchable by topic, category, or skill

### 🎯 Four Age Bands Per Topic
Every topic includes guidance specifically written for:
- **Ages 3–6** — simple language, concrete steps, caregiver-facing framing
- **Ages 7–12** — developing emotional vocabulary, pattern recognition, growing autonomy
- **Ages 13–17** — nuanced, treats teens as capable of real self-reflection, names the stakes
- **Adults** — full depth, complex dynamics, professional and relational context

Not every topic applies to every age band — where a topic only makes sense for older groups, earlier bands are simply omitted.

### 📋 Four-Part Guide Structure
Every age band within every topic contains the same four components:

**1. Step by step** — a clear, numbered walkthrough of how to approach and have the conversation, with coaching tips on each step

**2. What to say — and what to avoid** — side-by-side script pairs showing example language that works and the common alternatives that tend to backfire

**3. What to watch for** — specific signals, patterns, and edge cases that might require a different approach or additional support

**4. Reflection + Self-check** — two reflection prompts for after the conversation, plus an interactive checklist that scores how it went (with honest, encouraging feedback at each level)

### 🔍 Search
Full-text search across titles, categories, and skills — results update live as you type.

|-------|---------------|

| **Conflict Resolution** | Staying in hard conversations without causing destruction |
| **Vulnerability** | Saying the true thing even when it's scary |
| **Accountability** | Owning your part — specifically, fully, without qualification |
| **Authenticity** | Being honest about who you actually are |
| **Limits** | Knowing what you will and won't engage with — and holding it |

| **Forgiveness** | Releasing resentment for your own freedom, not their absolution |
| **Emotional Intelligence** | Understanding and working with your own emotional responses |
| **Repair** | Coming back to each other after conflict or distance |

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Requirements
- Any modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge)
- No server, build tools, or dependencies required
- Works offline after initial load (Google Fonts requires a connection on first visit)

### Installation

```bash
# Clone the repository
git clone https://github.com/your-username/conversation-guide.git

# Navigate into the project
cd conversation-guide

# Open directly in your browser
open conversation-guide.html
```

Or simply **download `conversation-guide.html`** and open it in any browser. No setup required.

## 💾 Data & Privacy

- **No data is stored** — The Conversation Guide uses no `localStorage`, cookies, or any backend
- Each session is completely fresh; nothing persists between visits
- **No accounts, logins, or tracking** of any kind
- No data is ever transmitted to any server

> This makes The Conversation Guide suitable for use in schools and clinical settings with no data privacy considerations beyond the device itself.

---

## ♿ Accessibility

- All interactive elements are appropriately sized for touch and click
- Color is never the sole differentiator — all tags and labels include text
- High-contrast text (Source Sans 3, bold weights throughout)
- Age tabs are clearly labeled with both short and long names
- No time-limited interactions anywhere in the flow
- `user-scalable=no` is set to prevent accidental zoom — remove from the `<meta>` tag if accessibility zoom is required for your setting

## ⚠️ Disclaimer

**The Conversation Guide is not a clinical tool.** It is a communication and self-reflection aid. It should not be used as a substitute for professional mental health support, therapy, or crisis intervention.

For topics involving safety — self-harm, dangerous home situations, abuse, or crisis — this guide provides a starting point for conversation, but professional support should always be sought. If someone is in immediate danger, contact emergency services or a crisis line.
